Discussion:

From the results, the europe-west1 instance clearly had higher latency than us-central1. This makes sense because I am most likely physically closer to the us-central1 region, so requests to europe-west1 have to travel a longer distance over the network, which increases the response time. I also noticed that the /register endpoint was slower than /list in both regions. That is expected since /register performs a write operation to Firestore, while /list only reads existing data.

In the eventual consistency experiment, I observed 0 misses out of 100 iterations. This means that during my testing, the data written in one region was immediately visible in the other region every time. Even though Firestore follows an eventually consistent model, the replication delay was small enough that I did not observe any inconsistencies in my loop. This still aligns with eventual consistency, since small delays can exist, but in this particular run they were not noticeable.
